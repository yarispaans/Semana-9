AVICII’S MUSIC SALES INCREASE 6,000% IN U.S. FOLLOWING DEATH

Since the tragic news broke that Swedish DJ Avicii had passed away aged 28 on Friday 20th April, sales of his music have increased 6000% in the U.S. On the day it was announced that the EDM star had been found dead in his hotel room in Muscat, Oman where he had been on holiday, there were over 25,000 downloads of his songs from online marketplaces, according to a preliminary sales reports by Nielsen Music.

MARTIN GARRIX DROPS BRAND NEW TRACK, ‘GAME OVER’: LISTEN

Martin Garrix has shared his new track ‘Game Over’. A collaboration with fellow Dutch producer, Loopers, Garrix announced that the track would be released today (20th April) earlier this week. The track debuted at Tomorrowland last year and was teased last week via a studio pic on Instagram. It’s the EDM stars second track of the year under his own name, having released his collab with David Guetta and Brooks earlier in the year.

TIËSTO: ‘SWEDISH HOUSE MAFIA REUNION INSPIRED AVICII TO GET BACK INTO DJING’

Avicii, before his death last Friday (20th April), had been inspired to get back into DJing by the Swedish House Mafia reunion at Ultra last month (March), according to Tiësto. The late Swedish EDM megastar, who died last Friday in Oman, retired from touring in 2016 due to health reasons. SW4 REVEALS STAGE BREAKDOWN FOR 2018 FESTIVAL

London’s SW4 festival has shared the stage breakdowns for its 15th outing this year. Taking place in Clapham Common on Saturday August 25th and Sunday 26th, the line-up for the festival features headliners like Armin van Buuren, DJ EZ, MK and many many more. Divided among five stages, there will be a lot of decisions to be made as to who to catch play for those headed to the event. Headlining the indoor mainstage on Satruday is trance legend Armin van Buuren, who is preceded by the likes of Galantis, Don Diablo and Example.
